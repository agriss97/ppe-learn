Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

What is AI? Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science focused on creating machines that can learn, reason, and make decisions. AI systems analyze data, recognize patterns, and improve with experience.

Learning objectives

  • Understand core AI concepts and terminology
  • Recognize major branches of AI (ML, NLP, CV, etc.)
  • See practical applications across industries
  • Appreciate ethical and safety considerations

Why AI matters

  • Automates repetitive tasks and speeds up decision-making
  • Improves accuracy in fields like healthcare and finance
  • Powers everyday tech: chatbots, recommendations, voice assistants
1. History & Evolution
  1. 1950s: Alan Turing and early ideas of machine intelligence.
  2. 1980s: Expert systems (rule-based AI).
  3. 1990s: Growth of machine learning methods.
  4. 2010s: Deep learning & neural networks scale up with data and compute.
2. Types of AI (by capability)
  • Narrow AI: Performs specific tasks (e.g., chatbots, recommendation engines).
  • General AI: Human-level problem solving (theoretical today).
  • Super AI: Surpasses human intelligence (future concept).
3. Types of AI (by functionality)
  • Reactive Machines: No memory‚Äîrespond to current input.
  • Limited Memory: Use recent data to make decisions (most current AI).
  • Theory of Mind: Models human thoughts/emotions (research stage).
  • Self-aware AI: Hypothetical; would possess self-awareness.

Core concepts

Data
AI systems learn from data ‚Äî quality and quantity matter.
Algorithms
Step-by-step procedures that enable learning from data.
Models
The result of training algorithms on datasets; used for inference.
Training vs Inference
Training = learning from labeled/unlabeled data. Inference = using the trained model to make predictions.
Branches of AI (quick guide)
  • Machine Learning (ML) ‚Äî Algorithms that learn from data (supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement).
  • Deep Learning ‚Äî Neural networks with many layers for complex pattern learning.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) ‚Äî Text and speech understanding (chatbots, translation).
  • Computer Vision (CV) ‚Äî Image/video understanding (object detection, medical imaging).
  • Robotics ‚Äî AI controlling physical machines.
  • Expert Systems ‚Äî Rule-based decision-making systems.
  • Speech Recognition ‚Äî Converting spoken words to text and intent.

Machine Learning basics

  • Supervised learning: Learns from labeled examples ‚Äî classification & regression.
  • Unsupervised learning: Finds structure in unlabeled data ‚Äî clustering, dimensionality reduction.
  • Reinforcement learning: Learns by trial-and-error using rewards ‚Äî used in games & robotics.

Neural Networks & Deep Learning

Neural networks are layers of interconnected nodes that learn representations of data. Deep learning uses many layers to capture complex features ‚Äî used for images, speech, and large-scale language models.

Applications of AI (real-world)

  • Healthcare: Disease prediction, medical imaging analysis
  • Finance: Fraud detection, risk modeling, algorithmic trading
  • Business: Chatbots, recommendation systems, automation
  • Transportation: Self-driving cars, traffic prediction
  • Education: Personalized learning systems, automatic grading

Advantages & Limitations

  • Advantages: High accuracy, speed, 24/7 operation, automation of repetitive tasks.
  • Limitations: Data bias, high development cost, limited creativity, risk of job displacement.
Ethics & Safety

Key concerns include bias in training data, privacy violations, unfair outcomes, and misuse. Mitigations: transparent algorithms, diverse/fair datasets, privacy-preserving methods, and clear governance policies.

Future Trends (short)

  • Smarter robots and assistants
  • Better healthcare diagnostics with AI
  • Wider adoption in autonomous vehicles and smart cities
  • More natural human‚Äìmachine interactions
